What is the Quality Assessor Tool?

The Quality Assessor Tool is an instrument to help you improve your website. The goal of the website is to provide you with a way to test the quality of your website. Simply asnwer the questionnaire to the best of your ability, and we give you a score based on your answers.

What criteria does the tool use to assess my website?

The criteria for assessing the website are as follows: content, function, structure, usability, navigability, performance, compatablity, interoperability, and security. These features are available in a quality website.

What is the scale for each question?

The grading scale for each question is based on the answer that you choose.

→"Strongly Agree" means that the feauture is found on your website and is useful.
Score: 5/5.

→"Agree" means that the feature is found on your website.
Score: 4/5

→"Neutral" means that the feature is available but hard to find. Or the feature is not there, but a similar one is in its place. It's the middle ground.
Score: 3/5

→"Disagree" means the feature is available but useless.
Score: 2/5

→"Strongly Disagree" means the feature is not available on the website.
Score: 1/5

→"Not Applicable" means that this feauture does not apply to your site.
Score: None

At the end of the assessment, all these scores will be added up for each question and give you the final score.

What does my score mean?

The score at the end of the questionnaire is numberic qaulity of your wesbite. The highest possible score is 135 which means the given website has great quality. Please note that each question has a specified weight to get this score.

How do I get my results?

You can view your results by going through the assessment and once you have looked over all of your selections, it will send you to the Results page.

How do I navigate between pages?

To navigate between pages, simply click the navigation buttons in the navigation bar above. If you are currently taking the assessment, click the back or next buttons located on the bottom of the page.
